<p><ul><li>Traditional Train Control</li><li>Communication Based Train Control</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The mainline rail signalling systems market is divided into two primary types: Traditional Train Control (TTC) and Communication-Based Train Control (CBTC). TTC relies on fixed signals and trackside components to manage train movements, providing conventional safety measures but limited flexibility. In contrast, CBTC employs real-time communication between trains and control centers, allowing for more dynamic and efficient operations. This advanced system enhances capacity, reduces headways, and improves overall safety by continuously monitoring train positions and speeds.</p></p>
