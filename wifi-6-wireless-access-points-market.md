<p><p>WiFi 6 wireless access points are categorized into dual-band and tri-band types, each catering to different networking needs. Dual-band access points operate on two frequency bands, typically 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz, providing a balanced approach for general usage and moderate traffic. In contrast, tri-band access points offer an additional 5 GHz band, designed to handle higher data demands and reduce congestion, making them ideal for heavy usage environments like gaming, streaming, and large households or offices.</p></p>
