<p><p>The contaminant remediation market is primarily divided into soil and groundwater segments. Soil remediation focuses on treating polluted land by removing contaminants or stabilizing them, often through techniques like excavation, bioremediation, or chemical treatments. Groundwater remediation targets the purification of contaminated aquifers, employing methods such as pump-and-treat, in-situ chemical oxidation, or permeable reactive barriers. Both markets aim to restore environmental health and meet regulatory standards, driven by increasing pollution levels and growing environmental awareness.</p></p>
